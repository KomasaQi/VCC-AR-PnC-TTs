\section{Conclusion}
The core challenge in anti-rollover control for tank trucks lies in the establishment of twin models to predict liquid dynamics. In this study, an anti-rollover path tracking controller is developed. Firstly, a single pendulum model considering lateral and roll inputs is constructed and coupled with the simplified semi-trailer dynamic model. Secondly, an optimization problem is formulated based on a multi-constraint model predictive control approach, and fast solving methods are employed to ensure real-time computation. Based on these developments, a vehicle-liquid coupling co-simulation platform is established to validate the proposed control scheme under three different scenarios: left turning, high-speed single lane change, and short-distance double lane change. Finally, the similarity principle of the experiment for tank truck models is analyzed, and the effectiveness as well as real-time performance of the proposed control scheme are verified through deployment and testing on a model semi-trailer tank truck experimental platform, reducing 42\% roll angle, limiting $|LTR|<0.75$ and sloshing angle within -20$\degree$-20$\degree$, and peak time consumption one control step less than 20 ms in the controller tested. 

Further studies will aim to achieve integration between cloud platforms and real-time vehicle control within the System of Coordinated Control by Vehicle-Road-Cloud Integration (SCCVRCI), enabling predictive anti-rollover lane-change planning. Moreover, integrated control of steering and differential braking is considered to enhance vehicles' rollover resistance.
